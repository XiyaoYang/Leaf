Frequent asked questions

How far back does the data go?

Data goes back to 2014, except for Labs, which goes back to 1997.

How can I get help?

You can ask questions through REDCap. Leaf Administrators will respond to your question in two business days.

What does "in the Same Episode of Care" mean?

"And in the Same Episode of Care" connects two concepts by their encounter ID, which means the two concepts happen in the same visit.

For example, patients who have lab results for the WBC test, and in the same episode of care are diagnosed with Enterogastris are different with patients who have lab results of WBC "And" are diagnosed Enterogastris, which means Enterogastris patients who have had WBC tests in any given time.

Concepts like "Demographics" and "My saved queries" are associated with person and do not have encounter IDs. Querying concepts without an encounter ID in the same episode of care may return confusing results.

Is there a place that lists your queries?

Yes, there is! Your query will be saved in the "My Leaf" section on the navigation bar and in the Concept List.

You can also save your query use “save query” button in the dropdown list of "New Query" menu.

How do I use logical "OR"?

The "—or—" tag will show up when you drag over the multiple concepts in the same query box.

Is there a glossary of terms available under each of the categories?

There is no such a glossary. Instead, you can either open the concept tree or use the search box to look for concepts.

For example, searching for diagnostic terms by ICD-9 or ICD-10 code will be much easier due to the large number of items in each terminology.

What is the meaning of the numbers beside the little green man?

The little green man is an estimated count of patients within a given concept.

However, these numbers are periodically generated snapshots and may not be exactly equal to real-time numbers.

What is the difference between "Diagnoses" and "Problem List"?

Problem lists are populated with confirmed diagnoses entered by providers.

Diagnoses are usually generated from billing system. They may represent real or suspected diagnoses.

For example, a patient who has a diagnosis of Tuberculosis in the concept "Diagnoses" may just have been billed for Tuberculosis screenings instead of actually having the disease.

Can I exclude certain criteria from my query?

There are two ways of doing this:

First drag over the concept you want to exclude, then either

-changing query box header to "Not Patients Who" or "And Not",

-or left click the concept in box and choose "Exclude" in dropdown menu.

If I want to filter patients with diagnostic code, do I need to use both ICD-9 and ICD-10?

It depends on how far back you want to your query to go.

If you are looking for patients in the short term, ICD-10 will be fine;

If you are looking for patients ina longer period of time, you will need to put in both ICD-9 and ICD-10.
